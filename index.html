Overview

Robot learning datasets are often curated for visual and semantic diversity, but contact-rich manipulation also requires coverage over hidden physical regimes. We study this issue in bimanual stopper removal, where the visual scene and task geometry remain nearly fixed while hidden pulling resistance changes.

Our results reveal force-regime downward compatibility: policies trained under higher resistance transfer reliably to easier regimes, whereas lower-resistance policies fail upward. We further show that a few high-force demonstrations can substantially improve hard-regime reliability, suggesting hidden physical-regime coverage as a practical data-collection principle for contact-rich robot learning.

Real-Bottle AP/NP Validation Videos

We further evaluate cross-condition transfer on a real wine bottle with a pumpable vacuum stopper. The atmospheric-pressure condition uses 0 pump cycles, while the negative-pressure condition uses 70 pump cycles before each trial, creating higher suction-related removal resistance.
